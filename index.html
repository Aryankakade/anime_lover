Chapter One

Why Anime Exists

Long before screens, people gathered around fires to tell stories — of monsters, of heroes, of ordinary hearts doing extraordinary things.

In Japan, those stories found ink and paper, and paper became panels, and panels learned to move.

What began as drawings became worlds — worlds where a lonely kid could find a family, where the weak could become strong, where grief could be survived one episode at a time.

Anime never asked to be perfect. It only asked to be felt.

And somewhere, in a room lit by a glowing screen, a girl named Mampi found a place that felt like home.

Stories end.

Heroes leave.

Dear Mampi,

Erwin's journey came to an end. Mine hasn't. Not yet. And neither has yours.

Whenever life feels difficult, I hope you remember the courage of the ones who never gave up, the hope of the ones who kept walking, the kindness of the ones who chose to protect others even when it cost them everything, and the quiet determination of every character who taught you that you're allowed to keep going.

Thank you for loving these incredible worlds the way you do — it's one of my favorite things about you.

Happy Birthday, Mampi.

May your own story become even more beautiful.

— Aryan ❤️
